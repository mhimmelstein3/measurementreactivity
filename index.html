Basic Measurement Reactivity Sim

Model Setup

Let’s imagine we have the following model for a treatment we want to estimate for a prettest posttest design

y2=β0+β1treatment+β2y1

If we have measurement reactivity, the problem is that the act of taking the measurement at time 1 (y1) acts as confounder, meaning we can’t know if β1 might look different if y1 were not measured.

A standard solution might be to omite the prettest and just do a between subjects comparison without any prettest as a covariate

y2=β0+β1treatment

One issue with this approach is it is possible that baseline scores interact with the treatment, and that becomes impossible to to measure

y2=β0+β1treatment+β2y1+β3(treatment)(y1)

Our approach uses a planned missingness design, where participants are randomized into a design where y1 measurements are either observed or missing, and Type B auxiliary variables are used to impute the missing values, eliminating the effect of measurement reactivity.

I sketched out a simple simulation with the following data generating model

  • K is the number of auxiliary variables
  • N is the total sample size
  • treatment is the treatment condition (0 or 1)
  • prettest is the measurement condition (0 or 1)
  • y1 is prettest measurement
  • y2 is posttest measurement
  • npre is the sample size in the prettest condition
  • β1 is the effect of the treatment (compared to control) when there is no pretest at the average baseline score (y1)
  • β2 is the effect of the prettest being elicited on the posttest in the control condition (no target treatment) at the average baseline score (y1)
  • β3 is the effect of the prettest score on posttest (set to 1 for simplicity) in the control condition when there is no prettest
  • β4 is the difference in treatment effect (change in β1) when the prettest is taken (vs when it is not) at the average baseline score (y1)
  • β5 is the change in the effect of the treatment (β1) for one unit increase in baseline score (y1) when the prettest is not taken.
  • β6 is the change in the effect of the prettest (β2) for one unit increase in baseline score (y1) when the in the control condition
  • β7 is the change in the change the effect of treatment for one unit increase in baseline score (β5) when the prettest is taken
  • Raux2 is the proportion of variance in y1 explained by auxiliary variables

The data generating process is based on

aux∼MVN(0,I) NOTE: for now this implies predictors are independent. We should manipulate this!

y1,i=∑auxi+ϵimpute,i

where σϵimpute,i=K1−Raux2Raux2

y2,i=β0+β1treatmenti+β2(prettesti)+β3(y1,i)+β4(treatmenti)(prettesti)+beta5(treatmenti)(y1,i)+beta6(prettesti)(y1,i)+beta7(treatmenti)(prettesti)(y1,i)+ϵy2

In the first version of the simple simulation I set

  • β0=0 mean of y2 in control + no prettest condition (set to 0 for simplicity)
  • β1=.3 (should be manipulated)
  • β2=.2 (should be manipulated)
  • β3=1 (does not need to be manipulated)
  • β4=−.75(β1), meaning that 75% of the effect of measurement “overlapped” with the treatment effect (should be manipulated and we should think about if this is the right way to do)
  • β5=−.1 (should be manipulated)
  • β6=0 (should be manipulated)
  • β7=0 (should be manipulated)
  • σϵy2=1 (for simplicity, does not need to be manipulated I don’t think)
  • Raux2=.33 (should be manipulated)
  • N=400 (should be manipulated)
  • npre=.4(N) (maybe manipulated?)
  • K=3 (maybe manipulated?)
